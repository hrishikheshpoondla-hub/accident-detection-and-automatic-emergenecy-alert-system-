# 🧠 Guardian: Technical Deep-Dive & Brainstorming Vault

## 1. The "Human-Centric" Algorithm
Most emergency apps fail because they are too sensitive. Guardian uses **Post-Impact Verification (PIV)**.

### The Logic Chain:
1.  **Linear Accel Spike (>25m/s²)** -> "Something happened."
2.  **Stationary Detection (3s window)** -> "The device isn't moving."
3.  **Step Detector Polling (0 steps)** -> "The user isn't walking away."
4.  **Confirm Accident** -> Start SOS.

## 2. Audio & Accessibility Design
We implemented a **Gradient Warning System**:
- **Level 1 (Voice):** Soft robotic voice warns the user. This is "Human-First" design—it avoids unnecessary panic.
- **Level 2 (Siren):** High-intensity alarm. This is "Environment-First" design—it alerts bystanders to come help.

## 3. The "Smart" Location Payload
Our SOS SMS isn't just text. It generates a **Dynamic Google Maps URL**.
- **Source:** Combined `GPS` + `Network` + `FusedLocation`.
- **Accuracy:** The app pulls the "Last Known" for instant speed, then refines it during the 10s countdown for "Precision Sending."

## 4. Brainstorming: Future Innovation Modules
### A. The "Voice Command" Cancel
**Concept:** In a crash, a user might not be able to reach the phone screen. 
**Brainstorm:** Use `SpeechRecognizer` to allow the user to shout "STOP" or "CANCEL" to abort the alarm if they are pins-and-needles but okay.

### B. Impact Audio Recording
**Concept:** The app starts recording audio the moment an impact is detected.
**Benefit:** Provides crucial evidence of what happened during or after the crash (e.g., hit and run).

### C. Low-Power Beacon
**Concept:** If the battery hits 5%, the app sends one final "Last Stand" SOS with current coordinates before shutting down.
